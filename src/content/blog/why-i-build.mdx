I graduated from IIT Roorkee with a 9.007 GPA and a gold medal in Production & Industrial Engineering. My parents were thrilled. My relatives immediately started asking about the UPSC exam.

I started a company instead.

That company — Trucks24 — connected transporters and factory owners in real-time. It made about as much money as you'd expect from a 22-year-old with no customers, no revenue model, and an unreasonable amount of confidence. But it taught me something that no GPA could: **the distance between an idea and a working product is enormous, and almost nobody actually crosses it.**

I wanted to be someone who crossed it.

---

## The second company was different

When I joined Powerplay as the second engineering hire in 2020, I wasn't a founder anymore — I was an employee. But the founding mindset doesn't turn off. Within two years, we'd built the Android app from zero to 500,000 users. 99.98% crash-free. A team of 20+ engineers I'd helped hire and mentor.

I was 24. I thought I knew what I was doing.

Then I quit to start Vignam.

## Building in silence

Vignam was an AI-powered 3D education platform. The idea sounds obvious now — put 3D simulations in front of students, let them actually *see* how a heart pumps or a circuit works instead of reading about it. In 2022, we were one of the only teams doing it seriously.

I built the first version alone. No co-founder. No funding. A laptop and a conviction that interactive 3D education could actually work at scale.

Three years later: $1M valuation, 20+ people, technology that reduced Time-to-Interactive by 50% and bundle size by 30%, a WebRTC platform running 200+ concurrent live classes, a RAG search engine over thousands of 3D simulations. Things I'm genuinely proud of.

And then I shut it down.

Shutting something down you built from nothing is a specific kind of grief. I'm still processing it. But that's a different post.

## Back to Powerplay — this time for AI

I rejoined Powerplay in October 2025. Different role, different era. India's construction industry is about to go through the same AI transformation that software development did — and Powerplay is positioned to lead it.

At **Powerplay CORE 26**, we launched what we called India's first AI Workforce for Construction. 40,000+ contractors in the room. The response was not what I expected: it wasn't skepticism. It was hunger. These are people who've been managing ₹50 crore projects on WhatsApp and Excel. The first time they saw AI that actually understood their workflow — you could see it in their eyes.

That's why I build.

---

## The actual answer

I don't build because of passion. Passion is what you feel at the beginning. What gets you through the third year of a startup that isn't growing, or the two-week sprint to fix a production bug you caused — that's something else.

I build because I think software is one of the few things that can genuinely change how a person's workday feels. A construction site supervisor who used to spend three hours on reporting now spends twenty minutes. That's two and a half hours back in their life, every day. Multiply that by 500,000 users.

That math is what gets me out of bed.
